About LivestockLink

Closing Nigeria's livestock market-access gap

Word-of-mouth doesn't scale

Piggery, poultry, snail and fish farming are some of the fastest-growing sources of youth employment in Nigeria today. But most farm-to-buyer connections still happen the same way they did a generation ago: through a neighbour, a church announcement, or a trip down a dusty road on a tip-off. That works at small scale. It breaks down the moment a buyer needs guaranteed volume from a specific state.

"Pick your state, pick your livestock, see who has supply." That's the entire idea.

LivestockLink exists to close that gap — a single, searchable directory where wholesale buyers can filter by state and livestock type, and where farmers can be found by buyers they've never met.

  • 01

    Buyers can't search

    There's no central place to find, say, every goat farm in Sokoto or every catfish pond in Delta — buyers rely on personal networks that rarely cross state lines.

  • 02

    Farmers stay invisible

    A well-run snailery in Akwa Ibom has no way to reach a buyer in Lagos without a middleman who takes a cut of the margin.

  • 03

    Trust is hard to verify

    Without listings, reviews, or verified profiles, both sides take on risk every time they do business with someone new.

  • 04

    Youth employment is on the line

    Livestock farming is a major entry point for young Nigerian entrepreneurs — but only if they can actually reach buyers at scale.
